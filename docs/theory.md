# Theory Background

This project studies instanton physics in the quantum mechanical double well potential using Euclidean lattice Monte Carlo methods.

---

## 1. The Model

The Hamiltonian is

$$
H = \frac{p^2}{2m} + (x^2 - \eta^2)^2
$$

After rescaling \( 2m = 1 \), the Euclidean action becomes

$$
S_E[x] = \int_0^\beta d\tau
\left[
\frac{1}{4} \dot{x}^2 + (x^2 - \eta^2)^2
\right]
$$

On the lattice:

$$
S_E = \sum_i\left[\frac{(x_i - x_{i-1})^2}{4a}+ a (x_i^2 - \eta^2)^2\right]
$$

with periodic boundary conditions and

$$
\beta = N a
$$

---

## 2. Instantons

The classical instanton solution connecting −η to +η is

$$
x_I(\tau) =
\eta \tanh\left(\frac{\omega}{2}(\tau - \tau_0)\right)
$$

where

$$
\omega = 4 \eta
$$

The classical action is

$$
S_0 = \frac{4 \eta^3}{3}
$$

In semiclassical approximation, the instanton density behaves as

$$
n \sim e^{-S_0}
$$

---

## 3. Monte Carlo Sampling

Configurations are sampled with probability

$$
P[x] \propto e^{-S_E[x]}
$$

using the Metropolis algorithm. Each site update proposes

$$
x_i \rightarrow x_i + \delta x
$$

and accepts with probability

$$
\min(1, e^{-\Delta S})
$$

---

## 4. Observables

### 4.1 Two-Point Correlator

$$
C(\tau) =
\frac{1}{N}
\sum_i x_i x_{i+\tau}
$$

At large τ:

$$
C(\tau) \sim
e^{-(E_1 - E_0)\tau}
$$

allowing extraction of the energy gap.

---

### 4.2 Instanton Counting

Instantons are identified by sign changes:

$$
x_i x_{i+1} < 0
$$

The instanton density is

$$
n_{I+A} = \frac{N_{I+A}}{\beta}
$$

---

## 5. Cooling

Cooling accepts only updates that decrease the action.

This removes UV fluctuations while preserving topological structures.

Monitoring instanton density vs cooling sweeps reveals
separation of quantum noise and semiclassical dynamics.

---

## 6. Instanton Liquid Models

### Random Instanton Liquid Model (RILM)

Superposition of non-interacting instantons:

$$
x(\tau) =\eta\left[\sum_j Q_j\tanh\left(\frac{\omega}{2}(\tau - \tau_j)\right)- 1\right]
$$

---

### Interacting Instanton Liquid Model (IILM)

Includes a minimal separation constraint

$$
|\tau_i - \tau_j| \ge \tau_{\text{core}}
$$

to model short-range repulsion.

---

## 7. Non-Gaussian Corrections

We interpolate between Gaussian and full action:

$$
S_\alpha =(1 - \alpha) S_{\text{gauss}}+ \alpha S_{\text{full}}
$$

and compute

$$
\Delta S =
\int_0^1 d\alpha
\langle S_{\text{full}} - S_{\text{gauss}} \rangle_\alpha
$$

The corrected instanton density is

$$
n_{\text{corrected}} =
n_{\text{gauss}} e^{-\Delta S}
$$

---

## 8. Output Data Files

All numerical results are written to the `data/` directory as CSV files.

Each file is self-contained and suitable for post-processing or plotting.

---

### 8.1 Euclidean Paths

Typical files:

```text
quantum_path.csv
cooled_path.csv
rilm_path.csv
iilm_path.csv
````

Format:

```text
tau, x
```

* `tau` = Euclidean time coordinate (i * a)
* `x`   = field value x(τ)

These files visualize:

* Full Monte Carlo configurations
* Cooled configurations
* Instanton liquid approximations

---

### 8.2 Correlators

Typical files:

```text
*_correlator.csv
*_correlator_avg.csv
```

Basic format:

```text
tau, C(tau)
```

Ensemble-averaged format:

```text
tau, mean, std_err
```

Where:

* `mean`    = ⟨C(τ)⟩ over independent configurations
* `std_err` = standard error of the mean

Used for:

* Energy gap extraction
* Comparison between cooled and uncooled ensembles

---

### 8.3 Cooling Evolution

Typical file:

```text
instanton_density_vs_ncool.csv
```

Format:

```text
n_cool, n_inst, density
```

Where:

* `n_cool` = number of cooling sweeps
* `n_inst` = number of zero crossings
* `density` = n_inst / β

Used to study separation of quantum fluctuations and instanton structure

---

### 8.4 Instanton Liquid Models

Files generated by RILM / IILM analyses:

* Path configurations
* Corresponding correlators
* Instanton densities

These allow direct comparison between:

* Full Monte Carlo results
* Semiclassical approximations

---

### 8.5 Non-Gaussian Density Correction

Typical outputs:

```text
qmidens_integrand.csv
```

Format:

```text
alpha, expectation_value
```

These files store the integrand

$$
\langle S_{\text{full}} - S_{\text{gauss}} \rangle_\alpha
$$

used to compute

$$
\Delta S = \int_0^1 d\alpha \langle \Delta S \rangle_\alpha
$$

The final corrected density is printed to stdout.
